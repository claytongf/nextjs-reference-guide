Senior and lead interviews include an open-ended design round ("Design a news
feed", "Design Google Docs"). Interviewers assess how you _think_, not whether
you recall one right answer. Use a structure so you never freeze.

## A framework (RADIO)

<Steps>

### Requirements

Clarify scope before designing. Functional (what it does), non-functional
(performance, offline, a11y, i18n, scale), and what's explicitly out of scope.
Confirm assumptions out loud.

### Architecture

Sketch the high-level pieces: components, data layer, server vs. client
boundary, rendering strategy (SSR/SSG/PPR), and how they communicate.

### Data model

Define the entities and the API/contract. Pagination shape (cursor vs. offset),
normalization, and what's cached where.

### Interfaces / components

Break the UI into a component tree. Define props and the state each owns. Call
out reusable primitives.

### Optimizations & deep dives

Performance (virtualization, code splitting, caching), accessibility, error and
empty states, and resilience. Then deep-dive whatever the interviewer probes.

</Steps>

## What signals seniority

- **Driving the conversation** — you structure it; you ask the clarifying
  questions instead of waiting.
- **Trade-offs over absolutes** — "cursor pagination because the feed is
  infinite and offset drifts as items insert."
- **Naming non-functionals unprompted** — a11y, i18n, performance budgets,
  observability.
- **Knowing where to stop** — you go deep where it matters and flag the rest.

## Common frontend topics to rehearse

Infinite scroll & virtualization, optimistic updates, real-time (WebSocket/SSE),
client caching & invalidation, offline/optimistic sync, design systems,
rendering strategy selection, and image/asset performance.

<Callout type="info" title="Think out loud">
  The interview measures communication as much as design. Narrate your
  reasoning, state assumptions, and check in with the interviewer — a silent
  genius scores worse than a clear communicator.
</Callout>

<Callout type="success" title="Tie it back to fundamentals">
  Every recommendation should trace to a principle from these tracks — rendering
  strategy, the server/client boundary, state classification, caching. That
  coherence is what reads as "lead".
</Callout>
